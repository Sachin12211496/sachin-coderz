Role in Rural Development

Why are these small units crucial for villages?

  • **Employment Boom:** They are the **biggest creator of jobs** in rural and backward regions. (They give work to local people.)
  • **Balanced Growth:** They help spread industry evenly, preventing all development from concentrating in big cities.
  • **Local Talent Use:** They use local resources and skills, leading to faster growth in specific areas.

Key Challenges (Problems)

  • **Money Shortage:** Businesses often face a **shortage of capital** (finance) and struggle to get bank loans quickly.
  • **Selling Difficulty:** They have poor marketing networks and cannot spend much on advertising to compete.
  • **Old Methods:** They usually rely on **old technology and machinery**, which reduces their efficiency and quality.

Government Support Systems

  • **NSIC (National Small Industries Corporation):** This provides **machines on lease** (rent basis) and helps with both raw material supply and product sales.
  • **DIC (District Industries Centres):** This is the **one-stop solution** for entrepreneurs in every district for registration and getting government approvals.

Wholesalers: The Supply Chain Bridge

Who are wholesalers and what do they do?

  • **Definition:** A wholesaler buys goods in large amounts from the factory and sells them to many retailers in small lots.
  • **Help to Manufacturer:** They buy the entire stock and store it, allowing the factory to keep producing without worry.
  • **Help to Retailer:** They make goods available quickly, give market advice, and offer **credit (udhaar)**.

Retailers: Fixed Shop vs. Moving Shop

  • **Itinerant Retailers (Moving):** They do not have a fixed place and move daily. Example: **Hawkers and Pedlars** (selling vegetables, clothes door-to-door).
  • **Fixed Shop Retailers (Permanent):** They operate from a fixed store. Example: Small Scale: General Store, Specialty Stores. Large Scale: **Departmental Stores** (all products under one roof) and **Chain Stores** (multiple identical branches).

GST (Goods and Services Tax): Key Takeaways

  • **Core Idea:** GST is one single tax on goods and services, replacing many old taxes (like VAT, Service Tax, Excise Duty).
  • **Benefit:** It removed the **cascading effect** (tax on tax), making the final price clear and generally reducing it.
  • **Motto:** Promotes the idea of **One Nation, One Tax.**
